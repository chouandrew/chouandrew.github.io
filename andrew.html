I Wonder


Me pregunto,
¿Qué color prefieres
cuando pintas el cuarto del bebé?
¿Naranja o amarillo?
Me pregunto,
¿Qué hay en la mente
cuando de repente descubres que la luna se llena?
¿Vas a aplaudir y gritar?
Me pregunto,
¿Qué tipo de canciones cantas
cuando te sientes feliz or triste?
¿Puedo escucharlas, o ejecutar un acompañamiento?

Me pregunto,
¿Qué le rezas a Dios
cuando pierdes la esperanza o la confianza?
Me pregunto,
¿Qué deseos haces
cuando la estrella fugaz titiritea a través del cielo?

Más, obviamente
aún más de tí
yo quisiera saber
Pero no lo haré
y no puedo...
La distancia se mantendrá
antes de que el alma se lastime.


She Fancies You


She fancies you

"Love" is not the word
It has been deeply abused, twisted
and conflated with
sex, fear, addiction, attachment, possessiveness
so much misunderstanding
so much misinterpretation
like the aroma of a morning coffee
polluted, with sugar and milk
Among all those she has none for you

It's just affection
LOVE, in the simplest form

As for everyone she loved
I'd say
She fancied them for
the gorgeous forma de caminar
the melodic palabras
the intelligent cerebros
the swift and subtle expresión
at their sonrisas

You are the only exception
no visible reason she has
to justify the affection
It's just you, the YOU she knows so little about.


What the heck is that sentiment, anyway?
What is that monstrous thing?

No es amistad
for we share few of our lives.

No es admiración
for you are never a mere maestra.

No es deseo
for I find no jealousy in my heart.

Two Days to See You


Two days
to see you; five days
to think of you.

An eager to talk to her?
the city asked,
its buildings half-shrouded in the haze.

No
At this time any words could ruin
the Purity
conversations of the pettiness
obstruct the soul
A ghostly image in the mind will do
And if there is one form
that carries the emotion
greatest power in a soul
that would be the poems
for they are made
from mere sentiments.

Cupid put an arrow in my heart
I forgot about the hurt
It is luck,
in its best sense.

Icelady

immobilized in the woods, olvidado

I imagine the day
when God made you
in that October, awfully gray
blizzards in heaven
storms on earth
He must have been crazy
or slipped the hands
accidentally or purposely
He put, into you
the ice, his crazy coldness

Sestina: Turn to Religion


Winds screaming, clouds massing, God is preparing a rain.
Life gets so hard, and he sees through my heart.
His face gloomed, and the rain made an instant stop.
“Come, girl.” He raised a forefinger with love.
So miserable that you looked, tell me the story. But no words,
could describe my bitterness. I begged only for forgiveness, for I never had faith.


“In me the girl never believes. To get saved, she needs to hold the divine faith.”
With solemn utterance, again, there came the rain.
Trees trembling with force, in the valley, a girl gave her words.
“Had love in the past, fate tore us apart, since then I have lost my heart.
Never wanted to possess a thing, for her I prepared only love.
Even Hope gave me the pain, once started, it would never stop.”


“Put out the fire, turn off the lights, quit dazzling me. Make it STOP!
Since when do you start caring me? For what do I need your divine faith?”
“For the girl is falling, edge of earth, brink of hell. Evil love,
of demons she would get. On her skin fire would rain.”
“I care about nothing. A girl fears nothing when she has no heart.”
“Damned soul! I will leave you rotten in darkness, give you no merciful words.”


“Merciful words?” I need none of that. I don't need mercy, nor any words.
Fire on the skin, knife in the flesh, endless sufferings wouldn’t stop.
The dilemma is like a saw, running to-and-fro on my bleeding heart:
One moment disappeared my confidence; one moment Hope again gave me faith.
Ah, with your supremacy you could throw a forty-day-forty-night rain,
but even the world is drowned, you could not destroy my relentless love.


“There’s no hope for the girl, to keep alive her impossible love.
Before it turns to hatred, find peace, make it immortal through my words.”
“It’s slashing my vein, blood falling like rain,
into boiling oil throwing my heart, burning in fire before the beatings stop.
She could save me but she left, along with her my strongest faith.
YES, you are always right, there could be no remedy for my injured heart.”


Oh, well. The girl yielded to her lord. Gone with God her poor heart,
and forgot the ghost that smashed her love,
The mighty power, to him she’ll always keep a sincere faith.
From the beginning she shouldn't let her know, but remain secret the twisted words.
Drop the grief, abandon the hatred, embrace the God, for all this has to stop.
Then, let the heartache, fade and vanish in God’s flooding rain.


A lifetime it needs to fix the heart; in her story the tragic soul spoke these words:
Once I sent my love to a girl, I fell into craziness and could not stop.
Only turn to religion can I regain my faith, discern the truth, and survive Fate’s wild rain.

El Amor y La Muerte


Todos mis pensamientos hablan de Amor,
y son tan variados,

One makes me wish for his lordship,
otro afirma que su dominio es locura,
otro en su esperanza me trae dulzor,
otro me hace muchas veces llorar;
And they only agree in asking pity,
trembling with the fear that is in the heart.

Por donde no sé
cuál razón elija;
y quisiera decir y no sé qué decirme:
So that I find myself in a lover’s fucking maze!
And if I wish to make them all accord,
I am forced to call on my enemy.
Señora Piedad, para que me defienda.

Lo que me ocurre en la mente, muere.
When I come to gaze on you, sweet and joyful;
and when I am near you, I can feel Amor.
Who says: ‘Run away, if you care about dying’?
El color del corazón muestra el aspecto,

Pues, desfalleciendo, dónde puede se apoya;
and in the vast intoxicating tremor
I heard the stones beneath me cry: “Muera, muera”.
Peca quien me viese así entonces

si al alma conturbada no conforta,
con sólo demostrar que de mi se duele,
por la piedad, que vuestra burla extingue,
que nace del aspecto muerto
de los ojos, que su propia muerte quieren.

Madness/Delusion


I saw a girl jumping on the steps
up down down up
dancing frenziedly as she sang, with the feet
left right right left
Her mind rotted with desire
Her soul defiled with filth

Shouted about the love she once owned
Well deep in heart she loved an illusion
pushing her into confusion
But "come on!" She cried to me,
What is love then, if it’s not delusion?

I saw her killing moths
treading on the innocent spiders
I could tolerate no more
left her in the delicate madness

I pray for her in the heart
May peace be with the mad girl someday

You’re right, I don't love/know the real you, but an illusion I’ve created in my mind. I have only seen a minor part of you. But, let’s say, I found BEAUTY in that minor part. That beauty is not limited in your appearance, your personality, or even your soul. That beauty is a part of Absolute Beauty (as put forward by Plato in Phaedo), like the beauty of wind, the beauty of trees, the beauty of universe, only taking a different form in you. And it is that beauty that I fall in love with. Above is a song written by John Lennon and Yoko Ono. Its lyrics speak well for my emotions.

Those poems are from my diaries, eleven days, eleven poems, so they also tell the full (hi)story of this love: At the beginning when I found out that beauty in you, I felt happiness ecstasy, as in the poems She Fancies You, Cutie, Two Days to See You, Take Me to the Moon; later on I felt the desire to know more about you, but instantly fell into misery, as in I Wonder, Icelady. As that kind of suffering grew, I felt desperation and a strong religious demand for consolation, as in El Amor y La Muerte, Sestina: Turn to Religion. My life was severely disturbed; I felt the craziness that only a lover could possess, as in Falling, Madness/Delusion. But at last, it’s all over. I killed all the desires and finally made the love immortal. El Fin suggested the death of the part of me who loves you. (I regard that personality as a female, so in those poems “the girl” “mad girl” and “she” all refer to me.)
